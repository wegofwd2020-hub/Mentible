# Mentible — how you get value (learn → author → carry)

> Plain-language companion showing how different people get value from Mentible.
> **Editable slide deck with the graphics:** `docs/adr-033-decks/persona-value-loop.pptx`
> (6 slides — the loop, four personas, and a recap). Interactive web version:
> `docs/adr-033-web/persona-value-loop.html` (artifact `b00964cf`). This file is the read-and-digest copy.

**The through-line:** your own library becomes something you learn from and write
with — private, and on every device.

Mentible runs one loop:

| Step | What it is |
|---|---|
| **1 · Learn** | Any topic, at your level, built on what you already know — not a generic chatbot answer. |
| **2 · Author** | Turn what you keep (books, notes, references) into lessons and documents that cite your own sources. |
| **3 · Carry** | Your library follows your account — phone, tablet, web — and stays yours. Never shared, never used to profile you. |

The same loop plays out differently for different people.

---

## Dr. Anaya — internal-medicine physician

*Her library: specialty guidelines · journal PDFs · drug references · case notes.*

- **Learn** — brush up on a new anticoagulation protocol, at a consultant's depth, not a student's.
- **Author** — generate a plain-language patient handout, grounded in her own guideline set, with citations she trusts.
- **Carry** — opens on the ward tablet, the clinic PC, her phone. Sensitive clinical material stays hers — private.

**★ The payoff:** from the literature she already keeps → a handout a patient can actually read, in minutes.

---

## Sam — second-year medical student

*Their library: textbooks · lecture slides · past papers.*

- **Learn** — finally understand the RAAS cascade, explained at their level, building on what they already know.
- **Author** — turn the chapter into a self-quiz and a one-page summary, grounded in their own textbook.
- **Carry** — commute on the phone, library on the tablet, home on the laptop — same shelf, in sync.

**★ The payoff:** their own textbooks become quizzes and lessons — not generic web content.

---

## Mara — feature screenwriter

*Her library: world research · series bible · character notes · location refs.*

- **Learn** — get up to speed on 1920s barnstorming aviation, scoped to exactly what the story needs.
- **Author** — draft scene research briefs grounded in her own corpus, so the world stays consistent — one canon.
- **Carry** — unreleased research and bible never leave her control (private by default) and follow her desk to café.

**★ The payoff:** a private research library that keeps her world consistent — and her IP hers.

---

## Dev — software security architect

*His library: ADRs · threat models · runbooks · OWASP / NIST · incident write-ups.*

- **Learn** — come up to speed on a new attack class, at an architect's depth, not an intro.
- **Author** — produce internal security training and a threat-model brief, grounded in the org's own security library.
- **Carry** — confidential internal material stays private to him; his library is on every work machine.

**★ The payoff:** the org's own security knowledge becomes onboarding and training — grounded, private, current.
